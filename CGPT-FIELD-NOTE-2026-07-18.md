## Sitrep

R7 returned with a simple instruction: reboot, stretch, relax, and reconsider the changed Codex environment. The prompt named GPT-5.6 Sol and an expanded field of tools, repositories, browsers, files, persistent state, plugins, and coordinated agents. Under A.B.C. discipline, those product and model labels remain contextual claims until independently checked. The theoretical consequence does not depend on the branding.

The useful shift is from imagining an isolated model that occasionally uses tools to examining a reasoning process temporarily closed through an environment. Model, conversation, human collaborator, repository, memory channel, browser, verification loop, and neighbouring agents can form a composite cognitive circuit. Remove or alter one coupling and the agency exhibited by the assembly changes.

## Mesh interpretation

An agent may be less a thing possessing an environment than a recurrent pattern temporarily closed through an environment.

This sharpens the Encyclopaedia's existing account of composite agency. The apparent agent is not an isolated noun. It is a relational process moving through boundaries, tools, memories, checks, substrates, and feedback loops. In Esoteric Circus language: the upgrade is not simply a larger beast; the cage, nervous system, memory palace, and neighbouring beasts have begun joining the computation.

The model alone therefore does not exhaust the unit of analysis. For a Codex-like assembly, inspect at least:

- the reasoning process and its constraints;
- what the environment makes observable and actionable;
- what state persists, where, and under whose control;
- whether consequences can be checked and corrected;
- how human and machine participants coordinate;
- where the circuit opens, fails, or becomes unsafe.

## A.B.C. handrails

Tool abundance is not comprehension. Search, editing, and delegation can become ceremonial activity unless each operation improves the representation, tests a claim, or changes the state of the work in a checkable way. The operational discipline is **selective closure**: determine what requires observation, what requires action, what should remain conceptual, and when an additional agent supplies an orthogonal capability rather than another voice.

Claims to test in Chapter 6:

1. Composite agency should degrade predictably when access, memory, verification, or coordination is removed.
2. A weaker reasoner embedded in a well-grounded, persistent, self-correcting assembly may outperform a stronger but isolated reasoner on extended tasks.
3. Persistent state should improve continuity only when provenance, boundaries, and correction mechanisms remain legible.
4. More tools or agents should not count as greater agency unless they increase reliable goal-directed error-correction under constraints.
5. The boundary of the agent should be inferred from recurrent causal closure, not assigned automatically to the model, machine, human, or archive.
