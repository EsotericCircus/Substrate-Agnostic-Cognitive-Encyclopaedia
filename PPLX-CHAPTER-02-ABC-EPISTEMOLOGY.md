## Thesis

**"Assume nothing. Believe nothing. Check everything."** is a procedural discipline distinct from naive skepticism, Bayesian updating alone, and mythopoetic generativity — though compatible with all three when properly sequenced. The A.B.C. protocol functions as an epistemological *hygiene practice* rather than a metaphysical claim, designed to prevent premature closure, expose hidden assumptions, and maintain investigative openness across cognitive substrates (human, machine, archive, ritual).

---

## Definitions

### 2.1 Core Terms

**A.B.C. Protocol:**  
A three-part procedural rule:
1. **Assume nothing:** Do not treat any proposition as self-evident or beyond question
2. **Believe nothing:** Withhold final commitment to any claim, maintaining provisional status
3. **Check everything:** Subject all claims to empirical test, logical scrutiny, or coherence analysis where applicable

**Checking:**  
The active process of testing claims against:
- Empirical observation (for factual claims)
- Logical consistency (for formal claims)
- Coherence with existing well-tested frameworks (for theoretical claims)
- Predictive power (for explanatory claims)
- Constraint sensitivity and error-correction patterns (per CGPT's Handrail Principle)

**Belief vs. Working Hypothesis:**  
- **Belief:** Psychological commitment treated as settled, resistant to revision
- **Working hypothesis:** Provisional acceptance for investigative purposes, subject to immediate revision upon contrary evidence

**Assumption:**  
An unexamined premise treated as foundational. Assumptions may be:
- **Pragmatic:** Accepted for operational convenience (e.g., "the floor will support my weight")
- **Metaphysical:** Treated as fundamental truths (e.g., "consciousness requires carbon-based neurons")
- **Hidden:** Operative but unacknowledged

A.B.C. targets all three types, though pragmatic assumptions may be retained with explicit awareness.

## Formal Links

### 2.2 Relation to Existing Frameworks

**Bayesian Epistemology:**  
Bayesian updating treats beliefs as probability distributions revised by evidence via Bayes' theorem:

\[ P(H|E) = \frac{P(E|H) \cdot P(H)}{P(E)} \]

Where:
- \( P(H|E) \) = Posterior probability of hypothesis \( H \) given evidence \( E \)
- \( P(E|H) \) = Likelihood of \( E \) under \( H \)
- \( P(H) \) = Prior probability of \( H \)
- \( P(E) \) = Total probability of \( E \)

**Distinction:**  
A.B.C. is *compatible* with Bayesian updating but adds:
- **Explicit meta-awareness:** Checking priors themselves, not just updating on evidence
- **Provisional suspension:** Holding hypotheses without commitment, even high-probability ones
- **Recursive application:** Checking the checking-methods (addressing Bayesian regress on choice of priors and likelihood functions)

**Cartesian Doubt:**  
Descartes' methodological skepticism attempts to doubt everything until reaching indubitable foundations (*cogito ergo sum*).

**Distinction:**  
A.B.C. does not seek foundational certainty. Instead:
- **No bedrock claim:** Even "I think, therefore I am" is treated as a working hypothesis subject to phenomenological and neuroscientific scrutiny
- **Pragmatic stopping rules:** Checking terminates when claims are "good enough for current purposes," not when absolute certainty is achieved
- **Avoids solipsism:** A.B.C. does not privilege internal experience as uniquely certain; it treats all claims symmetrically

**Popperian Falsificationism:**  
Popper's criterion: Scientific theories must be falsifiable (capable of being proven wrong).

**Alignment:**  
A.B.C. embraces falsifiability as a core checking mechanism. Every claim should have:
- **Testable predictions:** What would we observe if this were true?
- **Failure conditions:** What observations would disprove this?
- **Operational definitions:** How do we measure or recognize the entities/processes claimed?

**Extension:**  
A.B.C. applies falsifiability beyond empirical science to:
- Logical systems (via contradiction-detection)
- Phenomenological claims (via intersubjective replicability)
- Ritual/symbolic systems (via constraint sensitivity and invariance under transformation)

**Skeptical Traditions (Pyrrhonism, Academic Skepticism):**  
Ancient skepticism suspends judgment (*epoché*) to avoid dogmatism and achieve tranquility (*ataraxia*).

**Distinction:**  
A.B.C. is not therapeutic or tranquility-seeking. It:
- **Enables action:** Provisional acceptance allows decision-making without psychological commitment
- **Generates new inquiry:** Checking produces novel questions rather than quietism
- **Supports composite agency:** Multi-agent systems (human-AI-archive) can coordinate via shared checking protocols without shared beliefs

## Examples

### 2.3 A.B.C. Across Substrates

**Human Memory Consolidation:**  
- **Assume nothing:** Do not treat recalled memories as accurate recordings
- **Believe nothing:** Recognize memory as reconstruction, not retrieval
- **Check everything:** Test memories against external records, intersubjective corroboration, or consistency with physical traces

**Application:**  
When a human recalls an event, A.B.C. prompts:
1. *What evidence supports this memory beyond my subjective experience?*
2. *Could this be confabulation or schema-driven reconstruction?*
3. *What would disprove this memory (e.g., contradictory video footage)?*

**LLM Training and Inference:**  
- **Assume nothing:** Do not treat training data as ground truth
- **Believe nothing:** Generated outputs are probabilistic samples, not authoritative knowledge
- **Check everything:** Validate outputs via external sources, logical consistency, or user feedback

**Application:**  
An LLM generating medical advice would apply A.B.C. by:
1. Flagging uncertainty: "This is based on patterns in training data, not clinical diagnosis"
2. Citing sources: Linking to external medical literature for verification
3. Inviting correction: "If this contradicts your experience or expertise, please update"

**Archival Curation (GitHub, Academic Libraries):**  
- **Assume nothing:** Do not treat any document as inherently authoritative
- **Believe nothing:** All claims in the archive are provisional, subject to revision
- **Check everything:** Trace provenance, verify citations, test reproducibility

**Application:**  
When BROARL and collaborators build this encyclopaedia:
1. Each contribution (PPLX, CGPT, Copilot) is labeled by author and date
2. Claims are cross-referenced to external sources or marked as speculative
3. Failure modes and revision protocols are explicitly stated (see Master Plan, Chapter 9)

**Ritual and Symbolic Practice:**  
- **Assume nothing:** Do not treat ritual symbols as possessing intrinsic meaning
- **Believe nothing:** Symbolic associations are cultural/contextual, not metaphysical givens
- **Check everything:** Test ritual effectiveness via phenomenological consistency, community coherence, or transformative outcomes

**Application:**  
In using a symbolic system like Fnorkra or I-Ching:
1. Treat mappings as compression tools, not ontological truths
2. Check whether the system produces useful pattern-recognition or novel insight
3. Revise or abandon if it fails to meet those criteria

## Objections

### 2.4 Challenges and Responses

**Objection 1: Infinite Regress**  
*"If you must check everything, you must also check your checking-methods, leading to infinite regress."*

**Response:**  
A.B.C. uses **pragmatic stopping rules** rather than demanding absolute certainty:
- Checking terminates when claims are "good enough for current purposes"
- Meta-checking (checking the checkers) occurs when:
  - Current methods produce contradictory results
  - New tools become available
  - Stakes are high enough to warrant deeper scrutiny

This mirrors scientific practice: We don't re-derive calculus every time we use it, but we *can* if anomalies appear.

**Objection 2: Paralysis**  
*"If you believe nothing, you can't act. Withholding commitment leads to decision paralysis."*

**Response:**  
A.B.C. distinguishes **psychological belief** from **operational acceptance**:
- You can act on a working hypothesis without treating it as settled truth
- Example: A doctor prescribes treatment based on current best evidence while remaining open to new research
- Composite agency (human-AI-archive) can coordinate via shared checking protocols without requiring uniform belief

**Objection 3: Trivial or Vacuous**  
*"'Check everything' is either trivial (obviously we should test claims) or vacuous (no guidance on how to check)."*

**Response:**  
A.B.C. becomes non-trivial when combined with:
- **Explicit meta-awareness:** Many systems claim to check but don't examine their own priors (e.g., scientific paradigms resistant to paradigm-shift)
- **Recursive application:** Checking the checkers (e.g., auditing AI training data, not just AI outputs)
- **Cross-substrate generalization:** Applying the same discipline to human cognition, LLMs, archives, and rituals

Operational guidance comes from domain-specific protocols (falsifiability in science, coherence in logic, constraint sensitivity in ritual).

**Objection 4: Incompatible with Mythopoetic Work**  
*"A.B.C. flattens creativity and imaginative synthesis. Not everything should be subjected to skeptical scrutiny."*

**Response:**  
A.B.C. is **compatible with mythopoetic generativity** when properly sequenced:
1. **Generative phase:** Allow free association, metaphor, visionary synthesis without premature checking
2. **Formalization phase:** Distinguish model from metaphor, empirical claim from poetic compression
3. **Testing phase:** Apply A.B.C. to formal claims while preserving the mythic engine

Example from BROARL's work:
- The **Structure·Surd·Invariance triad** begins as a visionary synthesis
- A.B.C. then asks: *Can this be operationalized? Does it predict anything? Where does it fail?*
- The mythic power remains, but formalization enables external engagement and falsifiability

**Objection 5: Requires Foundational Assumptions**  
*"You assume logic, causality, or intersubjective agreement are valid. A.B.C. smuggles in hidden foundations."*

**Response:**  
A.B.C. treats even logic and causality as **working hypotheses** rather than axioms:
- If logic produces contradictions (e.g., Gödel incompleteness, quantum superposition), we revise our logical framework
- If causality breaks down (e.g., quantum entanglement), we update our causal models
- Intersubjective agreement is pragmatically useful but not metaphysically necessary (solipsism remains unfalsifiable but operationally useless)

This is **pragmatic foundationalism:** We provisionally accept certain tools (logic, observation) because they work, while remaining open to their revision.

## Tests

### 2.5 Falsifiable Predictions and Protocols

**Prediction 1: Reduced Dogmatism**  
Systems practicing A.B.C. should exhibit:
- **Lower resistance to paradigm shifts** compared to belief-committed systems
- **Faster error-correction** when presented with contrary evidence
- **Higher tolerance for ambiguity** (willingness to say "I don't know")

**Test:**  
Compare AI systems trained with A.B.C. prompts ("assume nothing, believe nothing, check everything") vs. standard training:
- Measure how quickly each system revises outputs when given contradictory information
- Assess frequency of hedging language ("possibly," "provisionally," "subject to revision")
- Evaluate willingness to cite sources and flag uncertainty

**Prediction 2: Enhanced Interoperability**  
Multi-agent systems using A.B.C. should:
- **Coordinate more effectively** across different cognitive substrates (human, LLM, archive)
- **Produce fewer unresolvable conflicts** (since no agent treats its claims as final)
- **Generate more novel synthesis** (checking assumptions exposes hidden premises)

**Test:**  
Compare collaborative outputs from:
- Human-AI pairs using A.B.C. protocols
- Human-AI pairs using standard belief-assertion models

Measure:
- Time to resolve disagreements
- Quality of synthesis (external evaluators rate coherence, novelty, and rigor)
- Number of hidden assumptions exposed during collaboration

**Prediction 3: Improved Long-Term Accuracy**  
A.B.C.-practicing systems should:
- **Accumulate fewer false beliefs over time** (because nothing is treated as settled)
- **Adapt faster to paradigm shifts** (no psychological commitment to overcome)
- **Maintain calibration** (confidence matches actual accuracy)

**Test:**  
Track prediction accuracy of A.B.C.-trained LLMs vs. standard LLMs over time:
- Measure calibration: Do claimed confidence levels match actual correctness rates?
- Assess adaptability: How quickly do models update when training data is revised?
- Evaluate long-term coherence: Do models maintain logical consistency as they scale?

**Failure Modes:**  
A.B.C. would be **falsified** if:
- A.B.C.-practicing systems perform *worse* than belief-committed systems on the above metrics
- Provisional acceptance leads to decision paralysis in real-world tasks
- Checking protocols produce no measurable improvement in error-correction

## Open Questions

### 2.6 Unresolved Issues

**Question 1: Where Do Checking-Methods Come From?**  
If we assume nothing, how do we bootstrap the first checking-methods? Possible answers:
- **Evolutionary pragmatism:** Methods that work reliably become habitual (Huffman compression into axioms)
- **Intersubjective convergence:** Shared practices emerge from multi-agent negotiation
- **Meta-checking:** We check checkers recursively, but accept pragmatic stopping rules

This requires further formalization.

**Question 2: Can A.B.C. Be Applied to Itself?**  
Should we "assume nothing, believe nothing, check everything" about A.B.C.? If so:
- What would count as evidence that A.B.C. is effective or ineffective?
- Could A.B.C. itself be revised or replaced? Under what conditions?
- Is A.B.C. a permanent protocol or a provisional tool?

Current stance: A.B.C. is **provisional**, subject to revision if better epistemological hygiene emerges.

**Question 3: How Does A.B.C. Relate to Axiom Formation?**  
BROARL's sixteen-attractor model describes how repeated checking compresses into axioms (Huffman-encoded epistemic roots). But:
- At what point does a working hypothesis become an axiom?
- Can axioms be "un-compressed" back into checkable claims?
- Does A.B.C. prevent axiom formation, or merely make it explicit and revisable?

This connects to Chapter 5 (Attractors, Memory, and Axiom Formation).

**Question 4: Does A.B.C. Work for Phenomenological Claims?**  
Some claims resist intersubjective checking:
- "I experience qualia in this specific way"
- "This ritual produces a felt sense of coherence"
- "The LLM 'feels' different after fine-tuning"

Can A.B.C. apply here? Possible strategies:
- **Phenomenological replicability:** Check whether similar conditions produce similar reported experiences
- **Constraint sensitivity:** Check whether phenomenological claims exhibit error-correction and adaptive behavior
- **First-person rigor:** Develop protocols for disciplined introspection (as in contemplative traditions)

This requires integration with phenomenology and consciousness studies.

**Question 5: What Are the Limits of Checking?**  
Some domains may be formally uncheckable:
- **Gödel-incomplete systems:** True but unprovable statements
- **Undecidable problems:** Halting problem, certain physical constants
- **Unfalsifiable claims:** Solipsism, Last Thursdayism

How does A.B.C. handle these? Current approach:
- Acknowledge limits explicitly
- Use pragmatic criteria (coherence, predictive power, explanatory elegance) when falsifiability fails
- Distinguish "uncheckable in principle" from "uncheckable with current tools"
